PROJECT MANAGEMENT

PRINCE2 and Agile Integration in Software Development: An Exploration of Opportunities and Challenges.

By Ewan Blake · DEC 8, 2023

Introduction

PRINCE2 and Agile are two methodologies that inherently focus on two different elements of project management; some could even say they contrast each other in certain elements, but both share characteristics that allow them to work effectively together. “Important to highlight, that some of the PRINCE2 principles are ‘very much agile’, namely continued business justification, learn from experience, focus on products, manage by stages, and manage by exceptions.” (Lloyd & Macrae, 2020). Where PRINCE2 caters to creating and managing a project in a controlled environment, Agile prefers a more fast-paced and adaptive approach, never set to one specific path. This difference allows for the opportunity of challenges and opportunities to exist, and it is important to understand where these respective elements lie, and how best to utilise them. Ensuring the process of using two systems integrated effectively first calls for an understanding of the systems separately. Understanding the principles set out by PRINCE2 and Agile allows the project manager (PM) to make executive decisions based on their understanding of how the frameworks should be used. Furthermore, this paper aims to highlight the need and possible demand for integrated system project frameworks such as this, challenges and opportunities of using these two systems together, before delving into scenarios where these systems have been used effectively, before finally wrapping up the ideas in a brief conclusion.

The Need For Integration

“Both methods have their strengths and added value but also bring their own culture and conditions” (J. Kusters, R., van de Leur, Y., G. M. M. Rutten, W., & J.M. Trienekens, J., 2017). It is important to understand that some of the significant challenges PMs face when using these two systems are most notably the contrast in the demand for a well-documented and structured system. Where PRINCE2 does not allow for significant change along the development of the project, Agile leans towards customer feedback and interactivity. According to Marcin Dąbrowski (2023), the budget of an Agile-pure project can be fixed, at the cost of making the scope flexible in growth, meaning that either the demands are lowered or raised. In light of PRINCE2, which asks for a fixed scope, this can be a challenge. However, due to the flexibility of both systems, a study carried out shows that despite the issues that arise using these two systems together, a thorough understanding of the individual project management methods “allows for optimum performance of product development teams within a controlled project environment” (Cppd, R., Mba, M., & Kearns, M. , 2008).

Opportunities of Integration

Handling Risk

A study carried out by Tomanek and Juricek (2015) acknowledges the potential for opportunities to arise from the integration of these two systems. “Enrichment of Scrum with selected practices from the heavy-weight project management methodology PRINCE2 promises better results in delivering software products, especially in global development projects.” (Tomanek and Juricek, 2015). The above addresses the possible synergy created by using these two project management methods, whilst also emphasizing the importance of leveraging elements from both systems to create one whole system. In this case, taking the known flexibility of Agile, and the structured approach of PRINCE2, combining them to not only improve the overall quality of the project delivery but focus also on improving the process of risk management by incorporating elements specifically from PRINCE2.

Another study carried out by Mousaei and Javdani (2018) only further strengthens this element being a potential opportunity. They go on to highlight potential weaknesses within the Agile(Scrum) methodology that could further increase the risk of project failures, and by extension how integrating with PRINCE2 and taking advantage of PRINCE2’s systematic approach to project management could help to mitigate or even nullify weaknesses created by Scrum.

In essence, the two studies collated above help to show that if time is taken for thoughtful discussion and understanding of how the two systems can be effectively integrated, the principles of both systems, though often contrasted, can complement each other in ways that allow for weaknesses often seen in the singular methodology used to be fixed.

Handling Of Stakeholder Support

Firstly, to emphasise the importance of stakeholder management, a study carried out by Dohnalová and Zimola (2014) addresses the importance of stakeholder management by comparing the importance of it equally to both project management and the company behind it as a whole. “An enterprise should exploit the potential collaboration with stakeholders to avail benefits to the enterprise and at the same time, detect potential threats and prevent them.” (Dohnalová & Zimola, 2014). The above text again mentions the threat of risk. Still, it uses it as a way to emphasise the importance of interactive and effective collaboration with the stakeholders and how working with them can negate potential risks ahead of time.

Overall, this study helps to show that stakeholder management should be treated with as much care, time and focus as it is shown by the overall company producing and running a project. By doing so, it allows for a significant increase in the project’s success, and the overall feasibility of the running project, and ensures that the stakeholder’s and project management teams’ goals are aligned and synergise well.

According to a study carried out by Raharjo and Purwandari (2020), they identified the handling of stakeholder management to be a significant hurdle when executing Agile-only projects. However, they identified that integrating Agile with PRINCE2 can help to address hurdles faced when applying Agile purely by itself. By taking advantage of PRINCE2’s structured-focus approach, it can help to complement Agile’s flexibility, and in the long run, contribute more effectively to ensure stakeholder management support is properly adhered to. This paper also allows for a better understanding in theory of how integrating these two methods of project management can address root concerns found within the Agile system.

In conclusion of this opportunity, the two studies surveyed here help to highlight the need for effective collaboration and exploitation of stakeholder management. By highlighting the possibility of potential benefits by doing so, proactively ensuring the mitigation of possible risks to the overall project, it also allows more credit and care to be given to stakeholder management and emphasises the importance of it to a level similar to that of project management itself.

Challenges Of Integration

Handling Of Conflicting Project Methodology Principles

Due to the conflicting approaches to project development and the principles that guide these two methods of project management, when integrating them, there are bound to be issues. A study carried out by (Sithambaram, J., Nasir, M. H. N. B. M., & Ahmad, R., 2021) helped to address these conflicting principles and the issues that could be faced when attempting to integrate these two systems. However, the paper does not specifically mention PRINCE2, but it helps to give an insight into the challenges faced. One such challenge mentioned within this paper was the organization and structure of the overall project which was considered the most challenging factor. The act of balancing the flexible qualities of Agile and the stiff structural processes of PRINCE2 can make it difficult to try to balance the two, ensuring that no one methodology is favoured over another, or in an extreme scenario, is completely disregarded for the more convenient methodology.

“Systems and procedures were unaligned, sometimes to retain the plan-driven linear approach of traditional PMMs, particularly a Waterfall PMM; while, sometimes the misalignment seemed arbitrary, especially for procedures.” (Wells, H.m Dalcher, D., & Smyth, H. , 2015). The statement above helps to highlight the desire to stay in a more comfortable environment using a more project-controlled environment like Waterfall (or PRINCE2). The statement also aids in highlighting a further struggle with handling principle conflicts. It is reasonable to assume that due to the integration of PRINCE2 and Agile requiring team members to be willing to adapt to a new management process and the shift in cultural aspects, the careful selection of team members for the project is considered highly. If selected members happen to have a strong preference for either Agile or PRINCE2, it could create unneeded resistance within the ongoing project.

In conclusion of this challenge, the conflicting identity and approach of integrating these two systems can introduce potentially alarming challenges. To traverse through said challenges, it is imperative that a deep understanding of both individual methodologies, including their strengths and weaknesses is had. Through this deep understanding, it is possible to integrate these two systems to create a project environment that takes full advantage of both systems’ biggest strengths when integrated.

Handling Of Conflicting Mindsets

A study carried out by Poniszewska-Marańda and Tyran (2022) consisted of the integration of PRINCE2 and Scrum (Agile) and while it presented a lot of positive factors, a potential challenge was able to be taken from this. The report suggests the creation of a traditional “contract”, which in turn might create a clash with the project manager potentially having the role of negotiator between customer and supplier mirrored with Agile’s approach.

Furthermore, the text goes on to emphasise further that the role of the project manager in an Agile-led project does not lead the project team but instead helps to support and facilitate their needs consistently throughout the project. This is seen as a conflicting contrast compared to how a project manager operates in a PRINCE2 project, where they are usually taking on a more commanding role.

In conclusion of this specific challenge, the integration of PRINCE2 and Agile does present a positive approach when using these two methodologies together. However, it helps to highlight elements of these project management structures that would require a deep understanding to either properly blend two project management roles or create a new one based on the understanding of the two systems’ blended principles. Furthermore, effectively handling conflicting mindsets requires a well-thought-out plan. It is important to emphasise communication, implement thorough training of these two systems integrated, and maintain a shift in culture if it is required; potentially by hiring new staff that fit the criteria and requirements of this integrated system.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the integration of Agile and PRINCE2 presents several opportunities and challenges within the software development sector. Due to the conflicting principles of Agile demanding a more flexible, fast-paced and collaborative approach, whilst PRINCE2 demands a more structured and thoroughly documented approach, requires exploration of how these two systems can be integrated for it to be seen as a type of successful project management.

Potential opportunities can be exploited by the use of integrating these two systems. Studies that have been covered in this report suggest the potential of integrating these two systems to allow for an increase in the end-quality of the project, an increase in the management of risk and being able to spot risks earlier on before they become a significant threat. Finally, the integration of these two systems allows for each to complement the other in terms of mitigating their weaknesses, allowing for an overall improvement in the effectiveness of these two integrated systems.

One of the significant opportunities that arose from the scenario of successful project management integration was an improvement in stakeholder management. Ensuring engaging and concise interaction and communication with stakeholders allows for project goals to be properly understood by all parties and helps reduce any potential risks that could come later down the line by misunderstandings.

However, there are potential challenges that crop up when integrating Agile and PRINCE2; specifically when attempting to handle the conflict in each system’s principles and the general cultural shift that comes when integrating two contrasting systems. Trying to balance the structured and documentation-heavy approach of PRINCE2 with the flexible and fast-paced approach of Agile generally creates challenges within the organization attempting to use it, as well as structural issues with the project itself and team hierarchy should be carefully put together. The reluctance of team members due to their deep-routed favouritism to abide by the integrated principles of the two systems emphasizes the importance of team members who are open to change, and adaptation and have a deep understanding of how the two systems are integrated in order to properly ensure they are following what is set out.

Overall, the integration of PRINCE2 and Agile in the context of software development is not a project management type that would easily fit all types of projects. Any organization with an interest in using these two systems together require a deep understanding of the two integrated systems in order to specifically mould it to their organizations unique needs. For example, the size of the project and the scope itself, the culture and mindset of the organization, and the already gained expertise of the members within the organization; what they are good at, what they struggle with. With the added aid of thoughtful thinking and experience in managing projects, a clear and concise exploration of the potential opportunities and challenges, specifically the significant ones that can be exploited and mitigated respectively, can help to steer the path that could end up being a successful, flexible and highly adaptable software development project.
